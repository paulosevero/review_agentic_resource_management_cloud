### Abstract

Mostly matchmaking solutions are based on centralised architectures, which result in issues such as low transparency, biasness, single points of failure, and control. In this paper, we built a decentralized matchmaking platform based on microservices, blockchain, and multi-agent AI systems, which interconnected various factors of a match in a system. Our matchmaking platform integrates components such as Inter-planetary File System (IPFS) for distributed and decentralized content storage, Solana blockchain for immutable record (provides better security), RabbitMQ for message queuing for efficient load balancing and asynchronous processing, and multi-agent architecture based on deep learning models. Through experimental evaluation, we demonstrate 15.5% improvements in the precision of the matches, 6.5× throughput improvement to centralized approaches, and 99.94% availability with fault tolerance. Our work contributes to decentralized AI systems and serves as a prototype for building a transparent, secure and robust matchmaking platform. © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

The advancement of the embodied AI-enhanced Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) increasingly relies on high-efficiency communication under the limited bandwidth of private factory networks. Semantic communication has shown potential in enhancing transmission efficiency by extracting task-relevant information, yet effectively coordinating semantic compression and computational resources in such resource-constrained environments remains a major challenge. This letter formulates the integrated optimization of semantic compression levels and computing resource allocation as a dynamic multi-agent decision problem. We propose an attention-based multi-agent deep reinforcement learning (MADRL) framework, which enables distributed robots to adaptively adjust semantic encoding strategies and resource usage in a coordinated manner. Simulation results demonstrate that the proposed approach significantly outperforms benchmark methods, achieving a 20% reduction in communication latency and an 13% improvement in task success rate under typical industrial network conditions.  © 2026 IEEE.

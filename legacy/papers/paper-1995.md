### Abstract

Ubiquitous computing has emerged as a catalyst for facilitating intelligent, context-aware services in many situations. However, current systems encounter difficulties such as dynamic contextual variations, suboptimal resource allocation, energy inefficiencies, and limited adaptability. To rectify these deficiencies, we offer Intelli UbiAware, an intelligent autonomic framework for context-aware ubiquitous computing and efficient resource management. The methodology consists of three stages: (i) the capture and modelling of multi-layer contexts from diverse data sources, (ii) adaptive decision-making through reinforcement-driven autonomic control, and (iii) fuzzy-based optimization for energy-efficient and sustainable resource allocation. Experimental validation is conducted via the Intel Lab Sensor Dataset and the SmartSantander IoT Dataset, with performance assessed based on latency, throughput, energy consumption, and resource utilization. Findings demonstrate that Intelli UbiAware attains 28% reduced latency, 22% enhanced throughput, 31% energy conservation, and 19% advancement in resource utilization relative to baseline models like LEACH and HEED. In summary, Intelli UbiAware offers a scalable, safe, and sustainable solution for advanced ubiquitous information systems, demonstrating significant relevance in smart cities, healthcare informatics, and intelligent automation.  © 2025 IEEE.

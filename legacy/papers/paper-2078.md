### Abstract

The Internet of AI Things (IoAT) is proposed as a novel transformative paradigm in which every connected device functions as an autonomous AI agent, collaborating over a peer-to-peer mesh to perform on-device perception, inference, and federated learning at the network edge. Departing from traditional cloud-centric AIoT models, IoAT embeds intelligence directly within resource-constrained endpoints-ranging from microcontrollers with neural processing units (NPUs) to graphics processing units (GPU)-accelerated edge servers-and interconnects them via a distributed AI mesh featuring a content-addressed model registry, federated learning coordination, and confidenceweighted inference federation. An adaptive Orchestration Layer leverages service discovery, software-defined networking (SDN)inspired quality of service (QoS) routing, and a domain-specific language (DSL)-driven policy engine to enforce service level objectives (SLOs) and elastic scaling, while a zero-trust security and governance fabric-built on mutual TLS (mTLS), hardware roots of trust (RoT), trusted execution environments (TEEs), rolebased access control (RBAC), and immutable Merkle-tree audit logs-ensures end-to-end confidentiality, integrity, and auditability. We present the first-ever formal IoAT architecture, describe a prototype implementation spanning diverse hardware platforms, and discuss prospective evaluation strategies to assess latency, bandwidth efficiency, privacy preservation, and resilience. © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

With the increasing application of artificial intelligence, cloud-edge collaboration necessitates deep integration to meet the demands of AI applications, including model training, deployment, and data collection. However, this integrated and collaborative system architecture also introduces unprecedented complexity, posing significant challenges for fault diagnosis, remediation, and reliability assurance in cloud-edge systems. This paper proposes a biologically inspired Autonomous Nervous System (BANS) framework for ensuring autonomous reliability. By mimicking the sensing, diagnostic, restorative, and learning mechanisms of biological nervous systems, it achieves comprehensive and intelligent fault management for edge device clusters. Experimental results demonstrate that this framework exhibits excellent performance in zero-shot fault diagnosis tasks, achieving an average diagnostic accuracy of 88.375 %, while also meeting the requirements for lowlatency inference. This research provides a novel approach for autonomous reliability assurance and intelligent fault tolerance in cloud-edge collaborative systems.  © 2025 IEEE.

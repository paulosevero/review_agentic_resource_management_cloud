### Abstract

Autonomous Mobile Robots (AMR) are increasingly used in textile manufacturing. Automated Guided Vehicles (AGV) are also being widely adopted. These technologies have led to significant improvements in automation. They have also enhanced operational efficiency in the textile industry. However, the next stage of industrial transformation-Industry 4.0-requires intelligent, data-driven optimization rather than mere mechanization. In this work, we propose an intelligent factory framework that integrates AMR and AGV robotics with an AI software platform powered by large language models (LLMs) and deep learning. The architecture includes edge robotics for material transport, distributed sensors for real-Time data, and a cloud-based AI engine. LLMs are used for semantic analysis, predictive maintenance, scheduling, and closed-loop optimization. The system processes multi-source data with advanced navigation and transformer-based models. We detail the model training process and implement the platform as scalable microservices, ensuring compatibility with MES/ERP systems. Industrial results show a 20% gain in efficiency, a 30% reduction in handling time, and a 25% drop in downtime. This demonstrates the potential of combining robotics, advanced sensing, and AI for smart manufacturing, and provides a basis for future research in intelligent textile factories.  © 2025 IEEE.

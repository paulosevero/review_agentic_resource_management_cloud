### Abstract

Since cloud computing is moving towards becoming the foundation of the key digital infrastructure, the necessity of smart and autonomous systems that can make decisions and optimize in real-time has expanded drastically. The typical cloud-based AI infrastructure has been mainly trusted to centralized orchestration and pre-written logic and thus was only agile when deployed within the confines of certainty and unchanging sets of requirements. The proposed study presents a new framework of Agentic AI in the cloud, which is aimed at allowing the development of self-directed, goal-oriented, consistently learning agents that could operate, manage, and optimize complex workflows with little to no human involvement. The suggested framework combines reinforcement learning, contextual reasoning and self-assessment loops to endow the agent with the capacities of dynamic goal prioritization, real-time adaptation and long-term optimization. Each agent will be an independent agent of control that perceives the world, requires the ability to make informed decisions, and resets its own behavior in the face of feedback and developing goals of the system.  © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

The convergence of aerial mobile computing and edge intelligence has increased the offloading of tasks to wireless networks, intensifying competition for edge resources. Lightweight edge intelligence enables efficient near-source computation, reducing burdens on Small Low-Altitude Aerial Platforms (S-LAPs) and improving scheduling. This paper presents a collaborative scheduling algorithm for edge systems with heterogeneous computational and communication capabilities. Industrial Internet of Things Devices (IoTDs) offload tasks to edge-enabled S-LAPs for parallel processing. We formulate an energy minimization problem that optimizes task partitioning, power control, and S-LAP trajectories, considering computational heterogeneity, wireless dynamics, offloading ratios, mobility, and transmission constraints. The problem is modeled as a multi-agent Markov Decision Process (MDP) with a composite reward function. A Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning (MADRL) algorithm is developed under an Actor-Critic framework, using dual estimation and target networks for synchronization. The approach employs centralized offline training and distributed online execution for scalable edge-end computation. Simulations show the algorithm outperforms existing baselines in convergence speed, task latency, and S-LAP trajectory optimization.  © 2025 IEEE.

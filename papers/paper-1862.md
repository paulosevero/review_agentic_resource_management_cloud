### Abstract

Mobile edge computing has become an important technological means of addressing the growing computational demands of user equipment in delay-sensitive applications. However, the presence of dependencies among tasks in these applications poses a significant challenge to the design of efficient task offloading and resource allocation strategy. We propose a scheme based on priority-sensitive types (PST) to jointly optimize task offloading decisions and the allocation of computational resources at the edge servers. This scheme introduces a task urgency definition that considers both time constraints and dependency structures. Based on this definition, we model task offloading and access point selection as a sequential decision process and solves it using the proximal policy optimization algorithm in the multi-agent deep reinforcement learning framework. Additionally, a dependency-aware greedy algorithm is designed to efficiently allocate edge server resources and dynamically adjust the offloading of certain tasks. Extensive simulation results show that the effectiveness of the proposed PPO-PST scheme significantly outperforms existing schemes in various scenarios in terms of long-term task execution efficiency.  © 2025 IEEE.

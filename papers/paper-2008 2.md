### Abstract

This paper introduces a two-stage operational framework for computation offloading in the mobile edge computing (MEC)-enabled cell free massive multiple-input multiple-output (CF-mMIMO) systems. The computation offloading task is divided into data transmission and task computing sub-tasks to achieve fine-grained optimization of energy efficiency and reduce task complexity. A staged multi-agent deep deterministic policy gradient (MADDPG) algorithm is proposed to solve the energy optimization problem. In the transmission stage, user equipments (UEs) act as agents to optimize the allocation of transmission power, while in the computing stage, access point (AP) clusters act as agents to optimize the allocation of computational resources. The simulation results demonstrate that compared with the baseline algorithm, the proposed scheme can achieve a significant improvement in energy consumption during the transmission stage, and near-optimal performance with lower complexity during the computing stage.  © 2025 IEEE.

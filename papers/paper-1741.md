**Abstract**

> The rapid growth and utilization of Large Language Models in large-scale data analytics have raised red flags regarding energy consumption and environmental impact. As more organizations start using LLMs to process large amounts of data, optimization of energy efficiency and carbon footprint reduction should also be made. The study develops ways to improve energy efficiency and reduce carbon emissions when deploying LLMs on real datasets like SQuAD and the Google Billion Words Corpus. We investigate the combination of model optimization methods, model pruning, quantization, and efficient training algorithms, which reduces the computational resources required for LLMs. We also evaluate the potential of using renewable energy sources and advanced cooling solutions in data centers to further reduce the overall carbon footprint.

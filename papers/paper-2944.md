**Abstract**

> With the rapid development of big data and artificial intelligence, the Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) has become a key enabler of Industry 5.0. However, the massive amount of data generated by IIoT devices imposes substantial computational burdens on networked systems. Mobile edge computing (MEC) offloads data to edge servers, thereby reducing processing pressure on devices and lowering system cost. Nonetheless, frequent data exchange between devices and edge nodes increases the risk of data leakage and may result in serious privacy concerns. To this end, we propose LG-MAPPO, a privacy-aware task offloading approach that integrates a locally enhanced Gaussian mechanism with multi-agent proximal policy optimization. Firstly, task data are classified into public and privacy-sensitive subdata through a privacy determination mechanism. The privacy-sensitive subdata are then protected using local differential privacy without relying on any third-party entities. To ensure robustness and practical deployment, LG-MAPPO integrates adaptive sensitivity estimation and perturbation validation. Afterwards, this task offloading problem is modeled as a Markov decision process which jointly considers latency, energy consumption, and task failure rate. LG-MAPPO finally leverages deep reinforcement learning to obtain near-optimal offloading policies through distributed execution and centralized training. Experiments on real datasets demonstrate that our approach not only effectively safeguards privacy but also reduces latency and task failure rates while consuming less energy, confirming its practicality in industrial scenarios. © 2026 Elsevier B.V.

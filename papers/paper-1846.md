**Abstract**

> This study explores the application of generative AI in smart cities to predict urban energy distribution and traffic congestion. It proposes a framework that integrates edge computing, microservices and AI agents to generate data using Generative Adversarial Networks (GAN) and Variational Autoencoders (VAE). The results show a 22% reduction in traffic congestion, a 12% optimisation of energy use and a 35% acceleration of public safety response times, as well as environmental benefits such as an 18% reduction in CO2 emissions and a 25% improvement in energy efficiency. The system, based on a three-tier architecture and more than 200 microservices, reduced processing time by 45 per cent and achieved 99.99 per cent system availability. © 2025 IEEE.

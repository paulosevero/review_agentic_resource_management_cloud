### Abstract

In the age of Industry 4.0, Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) systems need sophisticated, decentralized, and adaptable decision-making skills. This research introduces an innovative framework using Multi-Agent Deep Reinforcement Learning (MADRL) to enhance resource allocation, dynamic job scheduling, and fault recovery in Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) networks. Each agent in the system represents an IIoT node (e.g., sensor, actuator, or gateway) and acquires localized policies through shared global input, facilitating collaborative intelligence without requiring centralized management. We use the Fog-IIoTSim dataset, a simulated industrial dataset that emulates network traffic, device interactions, latency fluctuations, energy consumption, and fault injection scenarios inside a fog-enabled IIoT framework. The agents undergo training using the MADDPG (Multi-Agent Deep Deterministic Policy Gradient) and QMIX algorithms, with their efficacy evaluated against conventional optimization methods, such as Heuristic Scheduling (HS), Centralized DQN, and Rule-Based Systems. Experimental findings indicate that our MADRL framework enhances task throughput by 23.4 %, decreases average task delay by 18.9 %, and lowers energy consumption per job by 16.7 % compared to baseline models. Furthermore, the system exhibits exceptional flexibility in fluctuating industrial contexts, including fault-prone and high-load conditions. © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

Large language models have demonstrated strong performance in natural language processing tasks, but their high computational and memory demands hinder deployment on edge devices. To address this, we propose a lightweight inference acceleration method combining three techniques: dynamic activation quantization, semantic-aware pruning, and input-adaptive pruning scheduling. The method dynamically adjusts quantization parameters based on per-layer activation distributions to mitigate accuracy loss from outliers. It further incorporates word embeddings and syntactic features to assess token importance, enabling fine-grained, semantics-driven token pruning. Pruning strength is adaptively adjusted based on input length, improving adaptability across scenarios. Experiments show that our method achieves up to 2.8 × inference speedup without compromising model performance, outperforming standalone weight quantization or attention pruning.  © 2025 IEEE.

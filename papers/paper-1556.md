### Abstract

With the widespread adoption of the Internet of Things (IoT), many applications, such as self-driving and environmental monitoring, have created a surge in demand for lowlatency computing. This paper primarily focuses on information freshness, a critical aspect in real-time constrained scenarios. Initially, we model the MEC system as a Markov decision process and formulate an optimization problem to minimize the age of information (AoI) within the system. Subsequently, we propose an adaptive federal multi-agent soft actor-critic (AF-MASAC) framework for resource scheduling in UAV-assisted systems, which utilizes maximum entropy to solve the aforementioned optimization problem. To further enhance collaboration among edge agents, we propose an innovative adaptive federated learning mechanism, wherein the cloud center can dynamically determine the federated matrix based on environmental factors to optimize learning efficiency. © 2025 IEEE.

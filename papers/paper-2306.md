**Abstract**

> In edge computing, fusing privacy-sensitive heterogeneous sensor data poses challenges in balancing utility, privacy, and efficiency. Existing approaches like zkFL and zkGPT fall short in end-to-end verifiable LLM-driven fusion for non-IID data. We propose a framework embedding ZKPs into adaptive LLM layers for secure multimodal fusion with provable privacy. Key contributions: (1) context-aware attention for LLM fusion; (2) custom zk-SNARK circuits for full verification; (3) dynamic edge optimizations reducing latency by 25%. Theoretical analyses provide 7 -DP bounds and convergence guarantees. Experiments on UCI HAR and CIFAR extensions show 91.8% accuracy, MI-AUC of 0.52, and 45 ms latency on Jetson Nano, outperforming zkFL by 3.5% in accuracy and 25% in efficiency.  © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

The overwhelming scale of large language models (LLMs) exhausts the on-device communication and computation resources in vehicular networks, limiting its application in performing inference for vehicle intelligence. To address this challenge, we resort to caching technique, specifically developing a novel collaborative LLM inference framework. In the framework, the road side unit selectively caches intermediate computational results of input tokens from vehicle users, whereby the redundant transmission and computation can be reduced. Next, we evaluate the communication and computation overheads requested for implementing the proposed collaborative LLM inference framework. Then, we formulate a mixed-integer non-linear programming problem to minimize the collaborative inference latency by jointly optimizing the allocation of communication, computation, and caching resources. Simulation results validate the superiority of the caching-empowered LLM collaborative inference framework over baselines in terms of latency and energy efficiency. © 1967-2012 IEEE.

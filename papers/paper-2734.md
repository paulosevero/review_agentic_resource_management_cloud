**Abstract**

> Smart home automation is being revolutionized by the incorporation of digital twin (DT) technology, which makes it possible for real-time monitoring, predictive modeling, and improved decision-making. This chapter examines the design and application of DTs in smart homes, with an emphasis on important layers including data collection, processing, connection, and storage. The study addresses the cloud-based infrastructures, edge computing, and Internet of Things (IoT)- enabled sensors contribute to intelligent automation and smooth data synchronization. Additionally the study highlights opportunistic sensing, semantic web technologies, and linked data principles as essential components for improving data interoperability and system responsiveness. To identify the best infrastructure for implementing DTs in smart home settings a comparison between cloud-based and fog-based solutions is performed. Cloud computing facilitates extensive data analysis and remote access, while fog computing ensures low-latency, localized processing for real-time automation. This study further presents a practical design and implementation of DTs for smart home approach by utilizing microservices and external Application Programming Interfaces for energy optimization, climate control, security monitoring, and appliance management. The proposed system employs a decentralized linked data structure and a hypermedia multiagent system to enhance scalability and adaptability. By leveraging artificial intelligence-driven predictive capabilities and advanced data integration methodologies, DTs offer an intelligent and adaptive framework for smart homes. The findings underscore the potential of such systems in optimizing energy consumption, improving security, and enhancing user experience through automated and data-driven decision-making. This chapter contributes to the ongoing development of smart home ecosystems, emphasizing the significance of interconnected digital environments in the era of IoT and artificial intelligence. © 2026 Elsevier Inc.

### Abstract

Introduction. The digitalization of the Agro-Industrial Complex (AIC) faces systemic challenges driven by the dominance of centralized cloud platforms, including the lack of data sovereignty, low fault tolerance, and poor adaptability to heterogeneous conditions, particularly in mountain territories. These barriers create risks for food security and hinder the technological modernization of farms. Research objective. To develop and scientifically substantiate the architecture of a unified decentralized digital platform for the AIC, designed for the effective implementation of the Food Security Doctrine and the adaptation of advanced agricultural technologies to local specifics. Research methodology. The methodological basis was a critical analysis of existing centralized platforms and their limitations. The theoretical foundation for the design was the concept of decentralized architectures (Edge Computing, Offline-First), the scientific principles of Yield Programming Technologies (YPT), and artificial intelligence methods for solving privacy issues (hybrid LLM training schemes) and expert knowledge translation (formal-ontological mechanisms). Research results. The article proposes and describes in detail the architecture of a decentralized digital platform, the core of which is an “agro-node” localized at the farm level. A functional model based on the integration of YPT and AI is substantiated, ensuring a transition to proactive, science-driven agricultural production. The role of the decentralized approach as a tool for ensuring data sovereignty, fault tolerance, and the formation of a verified “digital trace” of products is defined. Conclusion. The proposed architecture systematically solves the fundamental problems of centralized platforms and is adapted for the sustainable development of the AIC in complex conditions. The platform is a scientifically-based tool for implementing the Food Security Doctrine, capable of transforming the AIC into a highly efficient, sustainable, and transparent industry. © 2025.

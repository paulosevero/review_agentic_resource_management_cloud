**Abstract**

> With the rapid deployment of Internet of Things (IoT) edge devices, the need for ultra-low power, small footprint, and high performance hardware is on the rise. Very large-scale integration (VLSI) design is key to fulfilling these requirements by integrating millions of transistors on a single chip. But modern VLSI design is so complex, in particular for low-energy budget condition, that time, cost, and optimization impose on it an hard challenge. This paper introduces an innovative emerging approach for implementing the VLSI design automation system at the edge nodes of the network based on artificial intelligence (AI) that is suitable for designing the energy constrained IoT edge devices. The proposed flow exploits the deep learning, reinforcement learning, and evolutionary programming to enable the automation of main VLSI design stages, such as, floorplanning, placement, routing, logic synthesis, and power optimization. Leveraging AI, the system can learn from historical design patterns and adapt to new design objectives and then dynamically optimize the trade-offs among performance, area, and energy. One of the biggest benefits of this AI-driven automation, is that humans have to do less work and design cycle times can be sped up enabling the rapid prototyping and deployment of custom chips for various IoT applications. The framework employs power-aware design techniques that are capable to address the special challenges of edge devices, like low power budget, occasional energy theft, and small thermal dissipation. Sophisticated neural architectures and reinforcement learning agents are employed to efficiently traverse through large design spaces in order to find optimal floorplans while reducing energy consumption without compromising performance. Furthermore, the use of AI can achieve adaptive optimization, the system evolves design in an iterative manner, which is testified according to simulated or field feedback. Experimental results with typical VLSI benchmarks and case studies for edge computing applications, e.g., wearable health monitors and smart environmental sensors, show that the proposed approach offers orders of magnitude improvement in energy efficiency, design time, and silicon area coverage over traditional electronic design automation (EDA) tools. The findings demonstrate the future prospects of AI for the automation of VLSI designing with intelligent and contextually aware automation to cater the challenging needs of future IoT systems. This work is a step towards design methodologies for resource constrained environments and toward fully autonomous, AI-based, chip design paradigms. The above innovations are imperative for scaling, sustainability, and compute intelligence within the edge in the backdrop of burgeoning IoT ecosystem.  © 2025 IEEE.

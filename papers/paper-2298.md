### Abstract

Unmanned Aerial Vehicle (UAV)-assisted Mobile Edge Computing (MEC) is emerging as a viable technology for computation-intensive and latency-critical jobs in the future Internet of Things (IoT), driven by the proliferation of smart devices and the expansion of high-bandwidth applications. However, the surge in data volume places a heavy burden on the scarce radio spectrum. This paper explores a UAV-assisted edge computing framework that incorporates non-orthogonal multiple access (NOMA), specifically a framework where multiple UAVs are deployed as edge servers to provide computational assistance to terrestrial Smart Mobile Devices (SMDs). Three-dimensional (3D) trajectory design, power allocation, and joint offloading decision optimization problems are proposed to minimize the system latency. Due to their movable nature, UAVs and SMDs must be redeployed in accordance with the movement of SMDs in a timely manner. to address this associated dynamic issue. The best offloading approach is first determined theoretically and mathematically, and the SMDs are then clustered using a K-means based clustering algorithm. Then, for every UAV server, we develop a cooperative multi-agent Twin Delayed Deep Deterministic policy gradient algorithm (MATD3) and formulate a multi-agent extension of the Markov Decision Process (MDP) using centralized training and distributed execution to jointly determine the UAV flight trajectory and power allocation. The simulation results demonstrate that: (1) the proposed algorithm can achieve lower latency than other offloading schemes while satisfying the task fault-tolerant energy consumption; (2) the system latency of the UAV-assisted MEC-NOMA network is 14% lower than the Orthogonal Multiple Access (OMA) case when the decoding order and power allocation are implemented; and (3) the proposed MATD3 algorithm's optimization of the UAV trajectory reduces the total system latency by 6% compared to the two-dimensional (2D)trajectory. © 2025 Elsevier Ltd

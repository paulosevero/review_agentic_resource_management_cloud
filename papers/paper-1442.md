### Abstract

This article presents a literature review on the implementation and optimization of collaborative strategies in microservices architectures, with a focus on game theory and agent-based models approaches. As microservices systems become increasingly complex, effective collaboration between services is critical for performance and scalability. We explore how game-theoretic concepts and agent-based modeling have been applied to improve decision making, coordination between the microservices, and resource allocation in distributed environments. The review highlights key approaches, compares methodologies, and identifies gaps in current research to suggest directions for future work. ©2025 IEEE.

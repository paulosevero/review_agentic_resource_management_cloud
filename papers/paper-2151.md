**Abstract**

> The manufacturing industry is undergoing a change due to the Internet of Things (IoT) and artificial intelligence (AI) coming together to create data-driven, intelligent, and autonomous production systems. Throughout the entire value chain, artificial Intelligence of Things (AIoT) enhances operational effectiveness, product quality, and decision-making by combining real-time sensing, data analytics, machine learning, and networked devices. With an emphasis on current developments and real-world case studies, this chapter offers a thorough review of AIoT’s contribution to the transformation of manufacturing technology. The first section of the chapter examines the fundamental elements and enabling technologies—such as edge computing, cyber-physical systems, digital twins, and improved connectivity—that propel the adoption of AIoT in industrial contexts. Then, it provides a number of case studies from different manufacturing sectors that show how AIoT has been effectively applied to improve supply chain operations, quality assurance, energy management, and predictive maintenance. Each case’s main performance results, difficulties, and lessons are underlined. The chapter looks at upcoming trends like low-code AIoT platforms, generative AI, human-centric Industry 5.0, and blockchain integration for safe and open operations. In order to scale AIoT across many manufacturing contexts, it also indicates important research areas and obstacles that need to be addressed. The chapter ends with strategic thoughts on how manufacturers may use AIoT to create production environments that are more intelligent, robust, and sustainable. © 2026 selection and editorial matter, Ankit D.

### Abstract

Edge computing enables energy-efficient, highresolution Virtual Reality (VR) rendering by optimizing realtime processing at the network edge. This paper proposes a usercentered edge computing framework for multi-user VR scenarios by jointly optimizing channel allocation and task offloading to minimize local computing energy consumption while maximizing user satisfaction. Based on the constructed distributed partially observable Markov decision process (Dec-POMDP) model, we further propose a Multi-Agent Transformer (MAT) based joint channel allocation and task offloading solution, where cooperative agents employ hierarchical attention mechanism of Transformer, utilize sequence modeling, and optimize policies using shared actor-critic framework. Simulation results show that our proposed solution achieves a higher trade-off between local computing energy consumption and user satisfaction over the baselines. © 2025 IEEE.

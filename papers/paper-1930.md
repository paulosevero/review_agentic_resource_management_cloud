**Abstract**

> In cell-free mobile edge computing (CF-MEC) networks, dynamic task arrivals and time-varying channels cause an inherent trade-off between processing delay and energy consumption. Existing deep reinforcement learning approaches can optimize resource allocation but often suffer from slow convergence and limited adaptability in highly dynamic environments. To address this, we propose a Transformer-enhanced resource allocation algorithm (T-RA), which leverages the Transformer's ability to capture long-term temporal dependencies for joint optimization of offloading ratio and power control. A two-stage training scheme is adopted, where pseudo-label pre-training accelerates convergence and cost-aware fine-tuning directly minimizes the weighted delay-energy objective. Simulation results show that T-RA improves convergence speed and reduces total cost by 15% compared with cell-free policy gradient (CF-Fed-DDPG), respectively. Moreover, under varying task loads, T-RA consistently maintains the lowest cost and demonstrates stronger robustness. © 2025 IEEE.

**Abstract**

> Large Language Models (LLMs) have significantly impacted the new age of AI innovation - but at a steep environmental cost. However, there is a lack of integrated guidance on how to combine model-level as well as datacenter-level improvements effectively to reduce carbon footprints while designing and deploying an LLM on cloud. In this paper, we aim to start a discussion on the unification of Cloud and LLM sustainability as one by first citing examples from previous work and provide a comprehensive analysis of the different real-world strategies used for model-level(pruning, quantization) and datacenter-level(chip-framework, renewable energy) optimization by providing examples from Google Cloud, AWS and Be.Ta Labs. We also perform a quantitative demo using LLMCarbon of different training scenarios on Llama2-7B and report a comparative analysis to demonstrate that basic optimization can achieve upto 81% carbon reduction compare to baseline configurations.  © 2025 IEEE.

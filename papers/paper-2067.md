### Abstract

This study looks at using AI and quantum computing to increase the security and efficiency of financial transactions. The suggested system's hybrid architecture enables autonomous agents to work together to make decisions. Computer performance is enhanced and accelerated by quantum techniques. Grover's algorithm speeds up the process of finding the best solution and confirming transactions. Quantum key distribution (QKD) protocols are designed to be impenetrable and protect data. Agent-based utility models are even more flexible and scalable because they can adapt to the needs of the operational environment. The design places a high value on adaptability, ease of installation across multiple platforms, and compatibility with other systems. It has a cloud-friendly design. Real-world testing demonstrates that the new techniques are far more effective than the previous ones. They reduced transaction throughput by 64%, vulnerability exposure by 89%, and processing time by 42%. Additionally, user experience research indicates that when they are not required to assist as much, people are 37% more likely to complete a transaction. The findings demonstrate that the application of agent-driven systems to the development of business and e-commerce platforms can be altered by quantum technology. Future development of new digital transaction ecosystems will be made easier by the suggested solution, which concurrently addresses security, adaptability, and operational efficiency.  © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

The integration of agentic artificial intelligence (AI) with sixth generation (6G) wireless networks is examined by this survey, and how next-generation communications can be transformed by autonomous multi-agent systems is explored. We analyze the fundamental differences between traditional AI and agentic AI paradigms, emphasizing their autonomy, adaptability, and goal-driven nature, which are well-suited to 6G environments. The paper introduces 6G architectures enabled by agentic AI, including multi-agent orchestration, edge-cloud integration, and intent-based networking. We examine key applications across network operations, service delivery, and vertical industries to demonstrate autonomous network management, personalized services, and transformative applications in healthcare, the industrial Internet of Things (IoT), and autonomous systems. The technical aspects that are identified as critical challenges include latency, scalability, and interoperability. The reliability challenges include trust, explainability, and fault tolerance. The security challenges include authentication, privacy, and adversarial attacks. Finally, we outline future research directions and standardization requirements to realize the full potential of agentic AI in 6G networks. © 2025 IEEE.

**Abstract**

> In the context of Low Earth Orbit Satellite Edge Computing (LEO-SEC), the rapid expansion of satellite constellations and increasing concurrent demands in hotspot areas high-light the limitations of individual satellites. A single satellite often lacks the resources needed to handle task processing with both low latency and high reliability. As a result, the importance of solving challenges related to multi-satellite collaborative computing and task offloading has grown significantly. To address these challenges, this paper proposes the Decentralized Multi-Agent MAB Task Offloading (DMAMTO) algorithm, which integrates the incremental learning mechanism of the Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB) into multi-satellite collaborative computing. Based on the framework of a multi-agent repeated stochastic game, it achieves joint optimization of distributed collaborative satellite selection and discrete task allocation. Simulation results demonstrate that the DMAMTO algorithm enables autonomous collaborative computing in LEO-SEC scenarios, effectively reduces task offloading costs, and consistently outperforms benchmark algorithms in simulation scenarios involving the Starlink, Kuiper, and OneWeb constellations.  © 2025 IEEE.

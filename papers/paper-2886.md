### Abstract

With the improvement of Intelligent Transportation Systems (ITS), Vehicular Edge Computing (VEC), which integrates roadside computing devices, can provide essential computing and storage resources for road vehicles. However, the high mobility of vehicles and the limited processing capacity of Roadside Units (RSUs) bring significant challenges for service migration, particularly in real-time decisions and resource scheduling. To address these challenges, this study proposes a real-time service migration strategy that leverages Artificial Intelligence (AI) technologies specifically Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)to optimize Quality of Service (QoS) and reduce response latency. Firstly, to solve the real-time decision problem, Prioritized Experience Replay and Four-Trajectory Exploration (PERFT), recurrent neural networks, and attention mechanisms are incorporated into the Proximal Policy Optimization (PPO) algorithm, yielding the Prioritized Experience Replay and Four-Trajectory Exploration Proximal Policy Optimization (PERFT-PPO). Then, to meet the multi-agents resource scheduling demands under VEC, the Centralized Training with Decentralized Execution (CTDE) framework is integrated into PERFT-PPO, resulting in the Prioritized Experience Replay and Four-Trajectory Exploration Multi-Agent Proximal Policy Optimization (PERFT-MAPPO) algorithm, which coordinates resource allocation among agents while enhancing global long-term rewards. Experimental results demonstrate significant improvements in real-time decisions and overall system performance. Copyright © 2025. Published by Elsevier Ltd.

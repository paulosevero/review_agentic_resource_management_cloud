**Abstract**

> Cloud computing has become an integral part of modern IT infrastructure, providing on-demand and scalable access to computing resources. There is significant growth in cloud users, and over time, accurate security, fairness, and efficient distribution of cloud resources are crucial issues. In this paper, a policy-driven model like QoS-Aware Policy Model and Meta-Learning Ensemble Model (MLEM) learner framework is presented, which aims to provide an consistent allocation of resources and prevent resource degradation due to data breaches, unauthorized access, and other similar threats. The framework includes a reputation-based user profiling system, workload-predicting predictive analytics, and role-based access control (RBAC) for securing the infrastructure. This reliability is maintained by using some multi-agent negotiation strategies based on predefined service level agreements (SLAs). After load balancing, the classification of fraudulent reviews and fake ratings generated by malicious profiles is implemented by using Adaptive Auto encoders. Performance evaluations conducted through experiments on simulated cloud environments demonstrate that the proposed framework significantly enhances resource utilization, fairly differentiates customers in terms of access to resources among users, and is more secure than conventional allocation schemes overall. This approach offers a scalable and adaptable solution for next-generation cloud platforms.  © 2025 IEEE.

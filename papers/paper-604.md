### Abstract

In this paper, we propose a novel approach for optimal resource management in ultra-reliable low-latency communication (URLLC)-enabled Internet of Vehicles (IoV) networks. The framework includes mobile edge computing (MEC) servers integrated into roadside units (RSUs), unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs), and base stations (BSs) for hybrid vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) and vehicle-to-infrastructure (V2I) communication. We utilize asynchronous federated learning (AFL) approach to enhance the accuracy of the global model by considering the mobility characteristics of vehicles. The problem of optimal resource allocation is formulated to achieve the best allocation of frequency, computation, and caching resources while complying with the delay restrictions. To solve the non-convex problem, a multi-agent actor-critic type deep reinforcement learning algorithm called D-MAAC algorithm is introduced. Extensive simulations show the effectiveness of the proposed framework and algorithms compared to existing schemes. © 2023 IEEE.

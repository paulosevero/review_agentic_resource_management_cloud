### Abstract

We presented a methodology for incorporating large language models (LLMs) into artificial intelligence (AI) agents capable of analyzing sensor and maintenance data to improve predictive maintenance tactics. We built on that foundation by moving from theoretical modeling to actual deployment, with a focus on real-time monitoring, adaptive learning, and agent scalability in live industrial applications. The new design prioritizes continuous data feeding, temporal analysis, and dynamic feedback loops, allowing the AI agent to adapt to changing equipment behavior and operational circumstances. The agent responds to emergent failure modes autonomously by combining LLMs with time-series anomaly detection, edge-to-cloud orchestration, and few-shot learning approaches, eliminating the need for substantial training. This stage also assesses deployment scenarios across many industrial systems, focusing on latency, reliability, and edge AI integration concerns. We investigated multi-agent collaboration and the implications of autonomous AI decisions in industrial contexts, emphasizing ethical and interpretability considerations under real-time limitations. This article focuses on real system integration, providing essential insights into the operationalization of LLM-powered agents for industrial AI. © 2025 IEEE.

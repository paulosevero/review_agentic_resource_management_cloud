### Abstract

Reinforcement getting to know (RL) is a kind of machine getting to know approach that focuses on gaining knowledge of a policy that enables an agent to interact correctly in an environment if you want to maximize its reward. RL is being explored to be used in quite dynamic cloud computing environments because of its robust potential to provide regulations that could efficiently adapt to converting environments. The use of Adaptive Multi-Agent Reinforcement mastering (AMARL) in cloud computing environments is a promising method for reaching excessive ranges of device scalability, workload balancing, and fault tolerance. AMARL is an RL method that includes more than one marketer that could act independently to improve the general device performance. This method capitalizes on the strengths of the character agents, as well as the advantages of distributed architectures. Using AMARL in cloud computing environments remains an emerging region of studies; however it has the ability to revolutionize the way wherein cloud infrastructures are controlled. This paper presents an overview of AMARL and explores how it can be used to gain better levels of scalability, load balancing, and fault tolerance in cloud computing environments. Furthermore, a discussion of the challenges and research opportunities in this subject are supplied. ©2024 IEEE.

**Abstract**

> Computing resource management systems play a crucial role in modern data centers and cloud computing environments, and their reliability directly impacts business continuity and resource utilization efficiency. Traditional Root Cause Analysis (RCA) methods face significant challenges when dealing with computing resource management metrics and logs due to fluctuating performance, complex log structures, and difficulty in capturing anomaly patterns with high real-time requirements. Inspired by the success of Large Language Models (LLMs) in software system analysis, we propose a multi-modal framework designed explicitly for anomaly detection in computing resource management systems. It combines domain-specific knowledge of computing resources and introduces innovative multi-modal fusion methods and temporal modeling techniques to achieve accurate detection of computing resource anomalies. On real-world datasets from a large-scale computing center, our method achieves improvements of up to 7.19% in HR@K and 7.23% in NDCG@K over state-of-the-art methods, while also offering superior real-time performance and interpretability.  © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

Vehicular Edge Computing (VEC) substantially reduces processing latency for latency-sensitive applications by offloading computational tasks to roadside units (RSU). A key feature of VEC is its ultra-low latency response, enabling realtime applications in the next-generation Internet of vehicles. However, the vehicular mobility and limited RSU coverage may lead to frequent handovers, which severely degrade the real-time performance of tasks. To address these challenges, we develop a handover-integrated task offloading model incorporating a multistrategy fusion framework. We employ a multi-agent proxy policy optimization algorithm to jointly optimize offloading decisions, handover strategies, and transmission power allocation to minimize average task processing latency, thereby reducing the impact of frequent handovers on latency-sensitive applications. © 2025 IEEE.

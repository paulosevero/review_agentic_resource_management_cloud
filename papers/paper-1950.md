### Abstract

Arup has developed a cloud-based web application called Iris for performing enhanced climate risk assessments. Iris supports risk assessments across a variety of hazards, including future climate projections when appropriate. Assessments can be performed using archetypal vulnerability curves or component-based methodologies. One of the key challenges in climate risk assessments is the ability to perform them at scale with a sufficient level of accuracy. For many public and private organizations, the number of assets considered within the portfolio can reach thousands of assets. These assets may be concentrated within a particular city or region, but can also be geographically dispersed. At this scale, the diversity of the asset characteristics, in addition to the variety of local geographical characteristics, significantly increases complexity of the problem space. This paper presents a system architecture that was developed to address this issue. The system uses two key technologies to solve the scaling problem: Cloud computing and Generative AI. © 2025 The Authors.

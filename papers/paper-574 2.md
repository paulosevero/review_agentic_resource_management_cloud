### Abstract

Edge computing allows road-side units (RSUs) to assist vehicles for computation. However, for an emerging vehicular application that consists of some mutually dependent sub-tasks, one RSU that has an edge server can't afford such workload. Thereout, we propose a scheme: every vehicle currently selects an appropriate RSU to offload its application; then, the application is computed via collaboration of RSUs; once it is finished, the result is delivered back to the vehicle. After that, to reduce latency and increase reliability, we formulate a joint optimization problem that determines the details of offloading, computation, and result delivery. For finding a solution to this non-convex problem, we transform it into a Markov Decision Process (MDP) and address it through multi-agent deep deterministic policy gradient (MADDPG). By numerical evaluation, we show that the proposed scheme with MADDPG can significantly lower latency and enhance reliability in time-varying vehicular networks.  © 2023 IEEE.

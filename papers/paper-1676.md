**Abstract**

> The swift expansion and application of Large Language Models in extensive data analysis have sparked concerns over energy usage and ecological effects. As additional organizations begin utilizing LLMs to manage extensive data sets, enhancing energy efficiency and minimizing carbon footprints should be prioritized. The research creates methods to enhance energy efficiency and lower carbon emissions when utilizing LLMs on actual datasets such as SQuAD and the Google Billion Words Corpus. We explore the integration of model optimization techniques, model pruning, quantization, and efficient training algorithms to decrease the computational resources needed for LLMs. We also assess the possibilities of utilizing renewable energy sources and innovative cooling methods in data centers to further minimize the total carbon footprint. The findings indicate that these optimization methods can conserve as much as 40 © 2025 IEEE.

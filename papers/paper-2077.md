### Abstract

Six generation(6G) wireless technology provides better coverage, lower energy consumption and improve security all while being cost-effective. 6G network provided platform for newer technologies like multiple access methods, advanced methods for designs, channel coating network slicing various antennas, technologies, and cloud -edge computing and artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning (ML) models. The explosive use of AI and large language models (LLM) raised concerns about data privacy and security. To overcome privacy concerns researchers and practitioners have started widely employing federated learning methods. The fully homo morphic encryption (FHE) is the underlying mechanism for the success of federated learning techniques. FHE suffers from significant limitations ciphertext expansion leads to slower data transmission and processing speeds, and increased storage requirements. Furthermore, as a newer encryption scheme, HE's widespread adoption is limited, increasing the risk of undiscovered vulnerabilities. This paper discusses the security challenges with 5G and next Generation wireless networks. By leveraging NOBE, we propose framework not only enhances security but also facilitates advanced data processing capabilities without compromising user privacy. The findings underscore the potential of integrating quantum-resilient encryption techniques in future network architectures, paving the way for safer and more reliable communication systems. Additionally, this paper explores ways to mitigate the challenges of ciphertext expansion and processing delays associated with homomorphic encryption (HE).  © 2025 IEEE.

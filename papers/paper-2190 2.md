### Abstract

According to Industry 5.0, there should be a strong synergy between machines, robots, and humans, and decisions within the industry should be made with a high level of reliability and in real-time due to the dynamism of the environment and limited resources. In order to overcome the problems, the following article describes Hybrid Edge-Cloud Intelligent Computing Framework that implies Multi-Agents Reinforcement Learning (MARL) and Digital Twin (DT) systems. Decentralized MARL agents making local decisions quickly and yet coordinating them globally via cloud-assisted DT predictive simulation is the novelty of this work. This coarchitecture can provide a latency to decision under 50 ms, faulttolerant execution and its re-allocation of load across different industrial conditions. A co-simulation environment based on Simulink was created to test the framework on the data of smart manufacturing and predictive maintenance units. It is experimentally demonstrated that the suggested framework enhances decision accuracy by 14.7% and 32.5% relative to baselines of centralized and individual agents, minimizes average latency, doubles throughput, and maintains an above-95% decision accuracy across different workloads. The results indicate that the framework is not only computationally efficient when using fewer than 70% of resources, but also scalable, robust, and adaptable, thus, is suitable to the real-time functional requirements of Industry 5.0 applications. © 2025 IEEE.

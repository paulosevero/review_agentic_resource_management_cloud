### Abstract

To address the complexity from renewable intermittency and multi-energy coupling, this paper proposes a distributed energy management and trading framework for data center microgrids (DCMGs) integrating PV, wind, C2P, GT, and BESS for coordinated low-carbon operation. The system is modeled as a Dec-POMDP, where each data center agent optimizes storage and trading with dual objectives of cost and carbon reduction. A Model-Based Multi-Agent Energy Trading (MAMBET) algorithm is developed, combining a world model with real and simulated rollouts to improve generalization and efficiency. Compared with MASAC and MADDPG, MAMBET cuts costs by 10.10% and 6.42%, and emissions by 3.37% and 2.13%, proving its advantage in intelligent and low-carbon DCMG trading. © 2025 IEEE.

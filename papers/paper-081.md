### Abstract

The environmental, economic and social sustainability of the logistics chain is a today's major global concern. The Physical Internet (PI) is one of the most promising initiatives in this field where the aim is to create a global, open and unified system founded on the interconnection of logistic networks and the sharing of the various involved resources. In this paper, we propose a new framework for the Physical Internet in order to optimize the objects' flow and, at the same time, maximize their traceability in the logistics chain by exploiting real-time data and computing through the use of Internet of Things (IoT), cloud and fog computing, and intelligent agents technologies. The proposed framework, on one hand, leads to increased visibility and traceability all along the supply chain, and on the other hand, it allows an optimized physical routing while complying with cost, time and quality constraints. © ILS 2018 - Information Systems, Logistics and Supply Chain, Proceedings.

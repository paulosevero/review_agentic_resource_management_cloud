**Abstract**

> This study introduces a groundbreaking approach by integrating large language models (LLMs), interactive artificial intelligence (IAI), and edge computing to enhance healthcare systems within next-generation wireless networks. We consider a scenario where multiple Internet of Things (IoT) devices transmit their data through a reconfigurable intelligent surface (RIS) and are processed by edge computing nodes (ECNs) before reaching a central control unit (CCU) and an LLM-based server (LLS). Using an advanced IAI-LLM framework, including modular components like retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), we develop a strategy to minimize operational overhead, focusing on dynamic user-specific data routing (DUDR). We validate our proposed IAI-driven model (IAIM) using a deep reinforcement learning (DRL) algorithm and compare it against a traditional manual optimization method. Simulation results show that IAIM reduces overhead by 44.96% compared to manual methods and significantly improves network scalability, particularly with the inclusion of DUDR and an increased number of ECNs. The IAIM framework's priority-based weight selection further optimizes resource management and data flow, outperforming random weight methods in large-scale IoT environments. © 2025 IEEE.

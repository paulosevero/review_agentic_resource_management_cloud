### Abstract

Smart, sustainable resource management is more critical than ever now due to the increasing energy needs and operational complexity of cloud and edge infrastructures, especially IoHT networks. New Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL)-based approaches addressing energy efficiency, VM migration minimization, and service-level agreement (SLA) fulfillment in cloud and edge environments are thoroughly reviewed in this review. Few of the articles provide experimentally validated solutions with carbon-aware scheduling and URLLC-limited task offloading at the focus, though quite a few gives AI-based or theoretical models at the proposal stage. Through the use of comparative statistics and summary tables, a systematic examination of algorithms, performance metrics and methodologies is presented, 13.2% to 41% energy saving, up to 86.53% VM migration reduction, and SLA/URLLC violation rates down to 3% are all evidenced by experimentally validated models. Particularly, multi-agent and DRL solutions exhibit significant improvements in adaptive task allocation, SLA assurance, and energy conservation. To ensure greener, more responsive, and SLA-aware infrastructures in rapidly evolving cloud and edge computing settings, the research emphasizes the importance of experimentally rooted optimization methods and points out significant research gaps. © 2025 IEEE.

**Abstract**

> The field of industrial robotics is experiencing a paradigm shift with the introduction of deep learning in distributed robot solutions. These, in turn, allow models to be deployed, trained and inferred over multi-agent networks that facilitate real-time, autonomous decision making without centralized control. In this chapter, we discuss important solutions for speeding up deep learning with an emphasis on challenges, trends and prospects. The use of deep learning in the context of distributed robotic systems is a very interdisciplinary and innovative area of research and development. This includes the processes of deploying the training and inference of the deep learning models onto a network of agents to enable self-autonomous real time multi agent systems without the need for primary decision making. The world has seen a growing number of autonomous robots, bringing new coordination challenges for these machines. We pose a new and unique challenge: each robot has to make use of tasks that require multiple robots with different skills to help. There needs to be fair allocation of robot resources among all requester systems to ensure that all requesters are sufficiently resourced and able to accomplish delegated tasks. An increasing number of studies of deep learning focus on its application as a strong framework. However, there is a growing gap in such studies that focus on the speed up of the distributed robot’s systems. Hence, there is a focus on what is currently known about deep learning to help develop distributed robotic systems. The scope of this paper is to clearly depict the spread of deep learning research, by understanding the acceleration factors which led to it. © 2026 Amit Kumar Tyagi.

**Abstract**

> Uncrewed Aerial Vehicle (UAV)-enabled Mobile Edge Computing (MEC) leverages dynamic mobility and Line-of-Sight (LoS) connectivity to bridge coverage gaps in data transmission and processing. However, UAV resource limitations impede continuous service provision. Centralized task offloading further intensifies network congestion in high-density environments, which degrades the Quality of Service (QoS). To address these challenges, this paper proposes a collaborative task offloading method that integrates UAV mobility with Device-to-Device (D2D) communication to jointly optimize system energy consumption and task latency. First, multiple UAV agents interact with the environment to gather information on the locations and task requirements of mobile devices within their observation range. Meanwhile, the central agent monitors overall network conditions and device loads. Second, Deep Reinforcement Learning (DRL) dynamically formulates the movement strategies of UAV agents and the task offloading strategies of mobile device agents, while D2D communication provides computing resources for devices lacking edge services. Third, the agents execute actions based on the decisions made, then update model parameters using reward feedback, with multiple UAV agents learning the global movement strategy through parameter-sharing. Finally, experimental results demonstrate that our method outperforms other methods, in the fields of balancing energy efficiency and computational timeliness in the high-density and emergency environments. © 1975-2011 IEEE.

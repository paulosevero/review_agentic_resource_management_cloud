### Abstract

As Transactive Energy Systems (TES) increasingly adopt decentralized architectures, intelligent energy agents must operate efficiently on edge devices. Deploying these agents on affordable edge hardware, such as the Raspberry Pi, is a promising approach to extend their scope and democratize TES. However, this deployment presents challenges in achieving efficient coordination between devices. Tools like Kubernetes can help with container orchestration to automate deployments and scaling solutions, but its original distribution is too resourceintensive for constrained edge devices. This study proposes a TES architecture based on a Lightweight Kubernetes Distribution to streamline energy agent deployment on the Raspberry Pi. The architecture's performance is assessed by scaling the number of agents and analyzing execution time, resource usage, and system reliability. The findings demonstrate the feasibility of running TES simulations on affordable edge infrastructure, offering significant implications for smart grid research and enabling decentralized energy coordination in resource constrained settings.  © 2025 IEEE.

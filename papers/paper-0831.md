**Abstract**

> With the continuous expansion and complexity of urban power grids, traditional power control methods are difficult to meet the needs of multi-agent, multi-energy and multi-level in modern power grids. To address this problem, this paper proposes a multi-level and multi-agent collaborative control platform for urban-scale virtual power plants based on a cloud-pipe-edge-end fusion architecture. By introducing technologies such as cloud computing, edge computing and the Internet of Things, the platform designs a multi-level control architecture that includes functional modules such as physical information modeling, resource aggregation optimization, automatic power control and instruction decomposition. The innovation of the platform lies in its ability to enable the full process of adjustable resources on the load side of the virtual power plant to participate in the automatic generation control (AGC) and automatic voltage control (AVC) of the power grid, and integrates intelligent monitoring and traceability technology of electric carbon, providing real-time and efficient power control and carbon emission management. Through actual case analysis and verification, the platform is significantly superior to traditional systems in terms of supply and demand balance, response speed, energy efficiency optimization and carbon emission management, which improves the overall operation efficiency and stability of the power grid and provides strong technical support for the intelligent management of urban power grids. ©2024 IEEE.

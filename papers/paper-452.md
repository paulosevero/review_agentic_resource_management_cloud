### Abstract

Cloud computing paradigm promotes dynamic resource delivery as a pay-as-you-go basis for providing utility computing services. The elasticity, capacity to obtain dynamically, and release computer resources in reaction to demand, is a primary characteristic of the cloud. Also, the resources at data centers consume enormous amounts of electricity, which incur very high operating costs, leaving big carbon footprints as a consequence. The high energy consumption is not because of the number of computer resources in use, but also the inadequate use of these resources. Cloud Resource Providers (RPs) are also experiencing under- and over-provisioning of resources due to the unpredictability of job arrival. The cloud solves the problem of under-provisioning resources for running services, which results in the loss of prospective consumers during peak hours. Even over-provisioning of resources burns capital. The key to effective cloud implementation is the determination of the optimum resource pool size for reservation. This problem can be addressed by the autoscaling function of the cloud for efficient resource utilization. A huge quantity of energy is used by the server in the cloud data center for fulfilling the request; high operational costs and resource scaling delays are the primary roadblocks to successful resource scalability. To adopt the dynamics, uncertainties, Quality of Service (QoS) interference, and compromise goals presented in the cloud, there is a need for a mathematical model with the benefits of self-organization and self-sustainable autoscaling with associated algorithms. These issues are tackled here by a well-known ecological model based on predator–prey interaction. This research proposes to resolves the issues of resource provisioning and distribution by employing an autoscaling procedure based on aforementioned the predator–prey model. Furthermore, energy-related issues are addressed at the data center level through resource management, which employs ecological principles such as allometric scaling law and metabolic rate. To resolve the issues of resource scaling delay, a holonic multiagent system approach is proposed. © 2022, The Author(s), under exclusive license to Springer Nature Singapore Pte Ltd.

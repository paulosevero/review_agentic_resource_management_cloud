**Abstract**

> With the blistering development of edge computing, new horizons have opened in the field of the implementation of AI systems directly on edge devices without the need to rely on centralized cloud environments. This paper introduces the Cloudless Intelligence paradigm which is centred on creating autonomous AI agents that could operate fully within decentralized edge fabrics. We discuss the architecture blueprint of edge-native agents which utilises local computation, federated learning and lightweight inference models to support real-time decision-making, privacy friendliness and context sensing. Our suggestion of a framework combines decentralized communication technology, energy-efficient AI algorithms, and dynamic resource orchestration strategies to achieve self-guiding conduct that is independent of cloud connections. Such experimental performance characteristics on various IoT and edge use case are recorded to justify the viability and practical benefits of our cloudless agents in the aspects of latency, resilience, and energy. This study establishes the design foundation of next-generation AI systems which are self-sustaining, privacy-conscious, and are designed to run in an intermittently connected distributed environment. © 2025 IEEE.

### Abstract

This chapter explores the transformative role of generative artificial intelligence (GenAI) in next-generation wireless communication networks, particularly 6G. It introduces a taxonomy of generative models, covering key architectures such as generative adversarial networks (GANs), variational autoencoders (VAEs), diffusion models, flow-based models, autoregressive models, and large language models (LLMs). GenAI enhances wireless systems through applications in physical layer design, network optimization, resource allocation, traffic analytics, localization, and security. The chapter also explores emerging frontiers, including semantic communications, integrated sensing and communication (ISAC), digital twins, AI-generated content, mobile edge computing (MEC), edge AI (EAI), adversarial machine learning (AML), and trustworthy AI. Finally, it addresses regulatory and policy challenges to ensure ethical and secure deployment. By bridging theoretical advancements with practical applications, this chapter serves as a comprehensive resource for researchers and professionals in wireless communication, AI, and network security. © 2026 by The Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, Inc.

**Abstract**

> The AI-IoT-Enabled Smart Campus Framework (AISCF) is proposed in this paper as a theoretical framework to facilitate the adoption of artificial intelligence (AI) and internet of things (IoT) technologies in higher education for improving learning outcomes, operational capability, and sustainability. The framework was evaluated in three scenarios: adaptive learning in smart classrooms, monitoring student engagement and welfare, and energy-space optimization. Results demonstrate that the AISCF efficiently provides personalized learning support, manages the usage of resources, and creates proactive support systems for students. Good insights are grounded on the fact that 4-tier heterogenous architecture of sensing AI analytics application logic and sustainability governance can convert traditional campuses into smart educational ecosystems. Insights for institutions aspiring to implement smart campus solutions were that validation reinforced the core values of systems interoperability, ethical data use and modular scalability. Future work will consider deploying the AISCF in practice to collect empirical performance metrics, to improve privacy-preserving data architectures, and to investigate how it can be combined with new emerging technologies such as synthetic and generative AI, edge computing, and digital twins. This foundation enables future innovations which can advance the intelligence and responsiveness of smart campuses and hence provide more engaging and supportive learning spaces.  © 2025 IEEE.
